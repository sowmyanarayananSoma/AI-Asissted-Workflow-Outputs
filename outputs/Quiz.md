# Slack Training Quiz

## Question 1
What is the primary difference between a public channel and a private channel in Slack?

**A)** Public channels allow file sharing, private channels do not  
**B)** Public channels are open to everyone in the company, while private channels have limited access  
**C)** Private channels can be converted to public channels at any time  
**D)** Public channels support video calls, private channels do not

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Public channels are accessible to all members of the workspace and appear in channel lists, while private channels have restricted access and are only visible to invited members. Note that you can convert a public channel to private, but not vice versa.

## Question 2
Which Slack command should you use to notify only the active members of a channel (not everyone)?

**A)** @channel  
**B)** @everyone  
**C)** @here  
**D)** @active

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The @here mention notifies only active (currently online) members of a channel, while @channel notifies all members regardless of their online status. This helps reduce notification overload for team members who are away or offline.

## Question 3
What is the recommended best practice for reducing notification overload in busy Slack channels?

**A)** Delete all messages after reading them  
**B)** Use threads to keep related discussions grouped together  
**C)** Send all messages as direct messages instead  
**D)** Disable all notifications permanently

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Using threads helps organize conversations by keeping related replies grouped under the original message. This reduces clutter in the main channel feed and ensures that only thread participants receive notifications about the ongoing discussion, rather than notifying everyone in the channel.

## Question 4
How can you quickly search for messages from a specific person in Slack using search modifiers?

**A)** Type "messages from [name]"  
**B)** Use the modifier: from:@[display name]  
**C)** Use the modifier: sent by:[name]  
**D)** Use the filter: user=[name]

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Slack search modifiers help refine search results. The `from:@[display name]` modifier filters messages sent by a specific person. You can combine modifiers for more precise searches, such as `from:@jane in:marketing` to find messages from Jane in the marketing channel.

## Question 5
What happens when you mention someone in a channel they are NOT a member of?

**A)** They receive a notification immediately  
**B)** The message is automatically sent to their direct messages  
**C)** Slackbot asks if you want to invite them to the channel  
**D)** The mention is ignored completely

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** When you mention someone who isn't in the channel, they will NOT receive a notification. Instead, Slackbot will prompt you asking if you'd like to invite that person to join the channel so they can see the message and participate in the discussion.

## Question 6
Which feature should you use to temporarily disable all Slack notifications when you need focused work time?

**A)** Away status  
**B)** Do Not Disturb (DND) mode  
**C)** Mute all channels  
**D)** Log out of Slack

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Do Not Disturb (DND) mode pauses all notifications and @mentions for a specified time period. You can activate it by clicking the bell icon and selecting a time period, or by using the `/dnd X minutes` command. This helps you focus on deep work without being interrupted by Slack notifications.

## Question 7
What is the purpose of starring messages or files in Slack?

**A)** To share them with the entire team  
**B)** To create personal bookmarks visible only to you  
**C)** To pin them to the top of the channel  
**D)** To mark them as high priority for everyone

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Starring items creates personal bookmarks that only you can see, unlike pinned items which are visible to all channel members. Starred items can include messages, files, or even entire channels, and can be accessed by clicking the star icon in the top right corner of Slack.

## Question 8
Which integration or workflow feature allows you to automate repetitive tasks in Slack?

**A)** Slack threads  
**B)** Channel archiving  
**C)** Slack workflow automation and reminders  
**D)** Status messages

**Correct Answer: C**

## Question 9
What is the recommended naming convention for Slack channels to keep them organized?

**A)** Use random creative names to make them memorable  
**B)** Use abbreviations at the front to cluster related channels (e.g., team-purpose)  
**C)** Always use full sentences as channel names  
**D)** Name all channels with numbers first

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Using a consistent naming structure like "team-purpose" (e.g., eng-scrum, ops-sales) helps organize channels alphabetically so that related channels appear together in the sidebar. This makes it easier for team members to find relevant channels quickly and understand their purpose at a glance.

## Question 10
How can you quickly jump to a specific channel or start a direct message without scrolling through your channel list?

**A)** Use the search bar at the top  
**B)** Use Quick Switcher (Cmd+K or Ctrl+K)  
**C)** Type /find followed by the channel name  
**D)** Click on "All Channels" and browse

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The Quick Switcher (Cmd+K on Mac, Ctrl+K on Windows) is the fastest way to navigate Slack. Simply press the shortcut and start typing a channel name, person's name, or workspace name, and you'll see suggestions instantly. This saves time compared to scrolling through long channel lists.
